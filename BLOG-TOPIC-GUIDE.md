# Blog Post Topic Selection Guide

## Purpose
This guide ensures the Writing Supervisor selects compelling, non-generic topics that attract readers interested in esoteric, Hermetic, and consciousness-related subjects.

## What Makes a Good Topic

### The "scroll-stopper" test
A reader should see the title and think "wait, what?" — not "oh, another article about that."

### Good topics have one or more of these qualities:
- **Surprising connection** — Linking two things the reader didn't think were related (e.g., quantum entanglement and the Kybalion's Principle of Correspondence)
- **Counterintuitive insight** — Challenging what people assume about a topic (e.g., "The Emerald Tablet isn't about alchemy — here's what it's actually describing")
- **Current relevance** — A news event, scientific discovery, or cultural moment viewed through a Hermetic lens (e.g., a new neuroscience study on meditation and the Principle of Mentalism)
- **Practical mystery** — Taking something esoteric and showing how it works in everyday life (e.g., Robert Monroe's out-of-body techniques applied to managing anxiety)
- **Historical revelation** — Something most people don't know about a well-known figure or text (e.g., what Neville Goddard's early life actually looked like before he discovered imagination-creates-reality)
- **Pattern recognition** — Showing the same Hermetic pattern repeating across different scales (as above, so below) in unexpected places

### Bad topics (avoid these):
- Generic "What is Hermeticism?" explainers
- Listicles like "7 Hermetic Principles Explained"
- Surface-level "How to use the Law of Attraction" posts
- Retelling history without a fresh angle
- Topics that could be found in any Wikipedia article
- Anything that sounds like it was generated by asking "write about Hermeticism"

## Topic Categories (rotate through these)

### 1. Hermetic Deep Dives
Pick ONE principle or concept and go deep with a surprising angle.
- Example: "Why the Principle of Polarity explains why you love and hate the same person"
- Example: "The Kybalion's hidden eighth teaching (that nobody talks about)"
- Example: "What the Emerald Tablet actually says about time"

### 2. Modern Science Meets Ancient Wisdom
Connect a recent scientific discovery or theory to Hermetic concepts.
- Example: "Quantum decoherence and why the Kybalion said 'nothing rests' 2,000 years ago"
- Example: "Block universe theory says everything already happened — Hermes said the same thing differently"
- Example: "The placebo effect is the Principle of Mentalism working in clinical trials"

### 3. Consciousness Explorers
Profiles or teachings from Neville Goddard, Joseph Murphy, Robert Monroe, etc.
- Example: "Robert Monroe's 'Locale II' and the Hermetic concept of the mental planes"
- Example: "The one Neville Goddard technique nobody uses (but should)"
- Example: "Joseph Murphy's sleeping technique — why it works and the Hermetic reason behind it"

### 4. Current Events Through the Hermetic Lens
Take a news story or cultural moment and show what it reveals about human consciousness.
- Example: "Why everyone is arguing about [X] — a Polarity principle breakdown"
- Example: "The [event] reveals how most people think the world happens TO them, not THROUGH them"
- Example: "[Political/social trend] and the Principle of Rhythm — what history keeps proving"

### 5. Practical Hermeticism
Everyday applications that feel like life advice but are rooted in real teachings.
- Example: "How to stop reacting and start transmuting — mental alchemy for bad days"
- Example: "The morning ritual that changes your vibration (and why it works by Hermetic law)"
- Example: "Why 'sleeping on it' is actually a Hermetic practice"

### 6. The Simulation / Matrix Angle
Simulation theory, the holographic principle, and how they connect to "The All is Mind."
- Example: "If we're in a simulation, the Kybalion wrote the source code"
- Example: "The holographic universe theory is just Hermeticism in a lab coat"
- Example: "Why physicists keep accidentally proving the Emerald Tablet"

### 7. Historical Mysteries
Lesser-known history of esoteric traditions, figures, and texts.
- Example: "The real Hermes Trismegistus — what we actually know vs. what we made up"
- Example: "How the Corpus Hermeticum survived the fall of Rome (and almost didn't)"
- Example: "The alchemist who fooled a king — and what it teaches about inner vs. outer gold"

## Anti-Patterns to Check For

Before finalizing a topic, run through this checklist:

- [ ] Would a reader who already knows basic Hermeticism still find this interesting?
- [ ] Is the angle specific enough that you couldn't write the same post about a different topic?
- [ ] Does the title make someone curious, or does it sound like a textbook chapter?
- [ ] Is there a concrete insight or "aha" moment, not just information?
- [ ] Could this post spark a conversation, or would people just nod and scroll past?

## Title Guidelines

- **Keep it short.** 5-10 words ideal. Max 12.
- **Create curiosity.** The reader should need to click.
- **No clickbait.** Don't promise something the post doesn't deliver.
- **No colons as structure.** "Hermeticism: A Guide" is boring. "Why Hermes Was Wrong About Time" is interesting.
- **Use "you" sparingly.** "Your Anger Is Trying to Tell You Something" works. "What You Need to Know About Hermeticism" doesn't.

### Good title patterns:
- "Why [common belief] is [surprising opposite]"
- "The [number] thing about [topic] nobody mentions"
- "[Specific person/text] and the [specific concept] connection"
- "What [recent event] reveals about [Hermetic principle]"
- "How [everyday thing] is actually [esoteric concept]"

### Bad title patterns:
- "Understanding the [X]"
- "A Complete Guide to [X]"
- "The History of [X]"
- "[X] Explained"
- "How to Use [X] in Your Daily Life"
