## Strengths

- **The opening is strong.** "You want the relationship. The career. The financial freedom." — three short punches that land without feeling listy. Then the mirror line deflates the setup at exactly the right moment.
- **Desire vs. assumption framing is the core insight and it's handled well.** The Kybalion quote ("The All is Mind; the Universe is Mental") is dropped naturally, not announced as a lesson.
- **Neville Goddard integration is accurate and useful.** The distinction between "want the feeling" and "assume the feeling" is genuinely clarifying — this is the kind of nuance most manifestation content misses entirely.
- **The Assumption Audit exercise is practical.** The "I want this, but..." prompt is simple, specific, and immediately actionable. Good.
- **The 21-Night Reprogramming Protocol** avoids the typical "just visualize harder" trap. Dissolving the assumption rather than shouting over it with affirmations is a more honest and grounded approach.
- **"The Part Nobody Talks About" section** is a real strength. Questioning whether the desire itself is inherited adds a layer of honesty most posts in this space won't touch. It prevents the post from becoming another "just believe and it happens" piece.
- **Rhythm varies well.** Short sentences punctuate longer ones. Some paragraphs are one sentence. The pace feels intentional, not mechanical.
- **Closing reflection questions land softly** without being a worksheet or a summary.

## Minor Notes (non-blocking)

- The Kybalion quote could include a subtle attribution ("as the Kybalion puts it") rather than just dropping it in quotes — but the current version works fine for the intended audience who already knows the source.
- "The assumption has seniority" is a great line. Keep it.
