## Strengths

1. **Strong structural choice.** Line-by-line analysis of the actual text is exactly what this topic needs. Most "as above, so below" content never gets past the bumper sticker. This post does.

2. **Principles woven in, not announced.** The post never says "today we'll explore the Principle of Correspondence." It just shows the principle working through the tablet line. Readers absorb the concept without feeling lectured.

3. **Voice and rhythm.** Short sentences break up longer ones. "Grounds it. Gives it substance." "The tablet says gentle. Says ingenious. Says use your head." This reads like someone talking, not someone generating.

4. **Practical section earns its place.** "What You Can Actually Do With This" gives readers three concrete things to try (watch cycles, notice correspondence, separate with gentleness) without sliding into a bullet-point tips list. It flows.

5. **The closing lands.** "The rest of the book is worth reading" is a quiet, confident ending. Not a summary, not a call to action, just an invitation. Works.

---

## Minor Issues (non-blocking)

1. **Opening could hook harder.** "You've heard the line" is fine but safe. The post jumps into the tablet immediately, which is efficient — but a relatable daily-life scenario before pivoting to the tablet (as the style guide suggests) might draw in readers who don't already care about Hermeticism.

2. **Slight tonal inconsistency in the opener.** The post opens by saying the tablet "reads like someone encrypted a recipe and threw away the key" — implying it's impenetrable — then closes by saying you can "memorize it in an afternoon." The contradiction is minor and doesn't undermine the piece, but a smoother transition between these two ideas would help.

3. **Title is functional but generic.** "Says More Than You Think" is a common blog title frame. It works, but it doesn't distinguish this post from a hundred other "you're wrong about X" pieces. Something more specific to the line-by-line format might land better.

---

## Summary

Clean post. Solid Hermetic accuracy. Good voice. Passes all hard criteria with room to spare. The three minor issues are polish-level, not structural. Ready to publish with optional title refinement.
