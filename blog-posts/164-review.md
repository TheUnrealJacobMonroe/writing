## Humanizer Check

### Issues Found

**1. Em Dash Overuse (~10 instances)**
Lines affected throughout. Some are working (punchy effect), but others could be commas or periods:

- Line ~7: `Not anything you can name. Not anything they said or did. Just a low hum of wrongness somewhere around your solar plexus — like a radio picking up a station that shouldn't exist.` → Fine, the dash works here.
- Line ~15: `Your brain processed micro-expressions, body language, vocal tone — all below the threshold of awareness — and synthesized a conclusion.` → Could drop the parenthetical.
- Line ~22: `Half a billion nerve cells, wrapped around your intestines in a network so complex that scientists named it the enteric nervous system — and started calling it your "second brain."` → Fine.

Most of the em dashes are doing real work here (setting up rhythm, adding aside info). **Recommendation: reduce by 2-3 where a comma or period would land just as well.** Don't strip them all — this voice uses dashes, and that's fine.

**2. Negative Parallelism (2 instances)**

- `You're not imagining the connection. You're not being woo.` — Line ~48. Minor. Works in context.
- `Not because it's mystical. Because it's real, it's specific, and it works.` — Line ~96. Rule of three tacked on ("real, specific, and works"). Could trim to "Because it works."

**3. Vague Attribution (1 instance)**

- `Neuroscience treats all of this as a curiosity.` — "Neuroscience" is doing a lot of vague work here. Who specifically? Could say something like "Most neurologists treat this as a footnote" or name someone.
