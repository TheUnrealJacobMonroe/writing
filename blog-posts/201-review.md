## Detailed Notes

### Strengths

- **Sharp opening hook.** Drops you straight into the problem — podcast hosts misusing physics. No throat-clearing, no "throughout history." Just cuts right in.
- **Tone is dead-on.** Reads like a smart friend explaining this over lunch. Dry wit ("the one person who actually studied physics quietly dies inside"), no preachiness, no guru energy.
- **The core argument is sound and genuinely useful.** The distinction between "consciousness collapses reality" (wrong) and "the universe IS mental" (Hermetic claim) is precise and the post earns it across several sections.
- **Section 3 and 4 are the best parts.** "That distinction changes the entire conversation" is a clean pivot, and the ocean/wave analogy works without overexplaining.
- **The close lands well.** "Start there." Two words. No summary, no "so next time you." Respects the reader.

### Em Dash Violation — Required Fix

Six in the body, one in the blurb (seven total). The limit is five. Two need to go or be replaced with commas, periods, or restructured sentences.

Affected lines in the body:

1. Line 45: `…every manifestation coach — all of it exists within Mind.`
2. Line 53: `…describes a real mechanism — interaction changes systems.`
3. Line 53 (same block): `…describes something else entirely: the nature of the thing that the mechanism operates within.` — *this one uses a colon, not an em dash. Fine.*
4. Line 61: `…at one scale — how interaction between systems produces change…`
5. Line 75: `…consciousness and quantum mechanics — people like Roger Penrose and Stuart Hameroff — are making far more specific…`

**Recommendation:** Convert the parenthetical on line 75 (Penrose/Hameroff) to a sentence: "The actual physicists who discuss consciousness and quantum mechanics — people like Roger Penrose and Stuart Hameroff — are making…" → "The actual physicists who discuss consciousness and quantum mechanics, people like Roger Penrose and Stuart Hameroff, are making…" That removes two em dashes in one edit (one pair). Body count drops to 4. Total drops to 5. Under limit.

Alternatively, replace one of the other single dashes with a comma or period and restructure the Penrose pair.

### Minor Observations (Not Failures)

- "Cargo-cult physics" on line 31 is vivid and earned, not filler.
- The "dinner party" paragraph walks a fine line between being a general scenario and a mini-anecdote. It works because it's a generic "you've probably seen this" situation, not claimed as personal experience. No action needed.
- Social media blurb could be slightly punchier (2 sentences instead of 4), but it's functional.
