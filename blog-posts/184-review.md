## Strengths

- **The dialogue is excellent.** The "hatred = love at high temperature / indifference = true opposite" opening is one of the best in the series. It hooks emotionally and teaches simultaneously.
- **The temperature analogy** is used precisely and never overextended. It grounds the abstract principle in physics without condescending.
- **The Living Application section** is practical without being clinical. The three domains (relationships, work, health) each have a clear pole pair and a concrete micro-habit.
- **The Polarity Shift exercise** is well-structured. The four-step process is genuinely doable in 5–10 minutes. The "do not leap, slide" instruction is memorable.
- **The 7-day tracker** provides real structure without feeling like homework.

## Minor Notes (Not Failures)

1. **"On the same pole" language** in Living Application — "Irritation sits on the same pole as interest" could confuse careful readers. The point is that these are same-axis intensities, not opposites. The language works in context but is slightly less precise than "they share an axis." Not a factual error; just terminology that could tighten.

2. **One minor grammatical quirk:** "the person who hates you and the person who loves you both care deeply" — correct and clear, but "the person who" used three times in rapid succession reads slightly repetitive. Could vary with "someone who" on one of the instances. Very minor.
