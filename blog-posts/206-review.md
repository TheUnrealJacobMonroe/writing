## Style Notes

**Strengths:**
- Excellent opener. The "Instagram captions / YouTube videos / motivational poster" trifecta lands hard and immediately earns the reader's trust by calling out the fluff they're tired of.
- The cause audit section is the strongest part of the post. It transitions from theory to practice without feeling like a worksheet. The relationship/money/mood examples are specific enough to feel real, generic enough to be universal.
- "Like planting a seed and then digging it up every morning to check if it's growing" — great image. Sticks in memory.
- Closes strong. "Be one of the few" is earned by the 2,000 words before it, not just slapped on.
- Neville Goddard section correctly positions him within the Hermetic tradition rather than treating him as separate.

**Minor gripes (non-blocking):**
- Line 83 has four "Not always..." / "But..." fragments in a row. Read aloud, the rhythm works, but it's pushing the negative parallelism pattern. Could trim one fragment if strictness is desired.
- The sentence "Which is a shame. Because the Principle of Cause and Effect is the most powerful of the seven Hermetic principles" — a standalone "Because" fragment. Some editors hate this. I think it works for the conversational tone, but flagging it.
- "Hermes didn't say the universe has a script and you're reading your lines" — minor historical note: the Kybalion's authorship is disputed (William Walker Atkinson, not ancient Hermes directly). But in the context of the blog's brand (Hermetic wisdom, not academic history), this is fine.
