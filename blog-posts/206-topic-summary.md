# PBI #206 — Topic Summary

## Proposed Title
**The Principle Nobody Explains Clearly (But Everyone Quotes)**

## Category
Hermetic Deep Dives (#1) — 4th rotation

## The Hook
Of the seven Hermetic principles, the Principle of Cause and Effect is the one people quote the most and understand the least. "Every cause has its effect, every effect has its cause." It sounds obvious. It sounds like something a bumper sticker would say. And because it sounds obvious, most people skip past it without ever asking the question that makes it dangerous: if everything has a cause, and you are experiencing an effect, then what caused it? The answer to that question is the difference between people who feel like life is happening to them and people who are actively creating their experience.

## The Angle
This post does a deep dive on the Principle of Cause and Effect — not as a platitude, but as an operational framework.

**The surface reading:** Everything happens for a reason. Events have causes. This is the version most people stop at, and it is useless.

**The Hermetic reading:** The Kybalion does not say "events have causes." It says the universe operates on the principle of cause and effect at every level, including the mental plane. Your thoughts are causes. Your assumptions are causes. Your emotional states are causes. The world you experience is the effect. This is not wishful thinking. It is the Hermetic claim that mental causation is as real as physical causation, and it operates whether you are conscious of it or not.

**The Neville Goddard connection:** Neville's entire teaching is Cause and Effect applied to assumption. "Assume the feeling of the wish fulfilled" is a causal instruction. You are the cause. The fulfilled wish is the effect. The gap between them is the operation of the principle.

**The practical application:** The post gives readers a "cause audit" — a simple exercise where they look at three things currently happening in their life and trace each one back to a mental cause. Not blame. Not guilt. Just observation. "I am experiencing X. What assumption or belief produced this?" The exercise is uncomfortable because it works.

## What the Reader Walks Away With
A clear understanding of why Cause and Effect is the most powerful and most overlooked Hermetic principle. A practical "cause audit" exercise they can use immediately.

## Why RIGHT NOW
