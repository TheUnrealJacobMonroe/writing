## The Hook
In the last five years, neuroscience has quietly confirmed something the Kybalion stated flatly: your brain doesn't *create* consciousness — it *receives* it. The Default Mode Network (DMN), that web of brain regions active when you're "doing nothing," turns out to be the exact neural correlate of the Principle of Mentalism. When DMN activity changes through meditation, psychedelics, or flow states, people report the same experiences that Hermetic texts have described for millennia: dissolution of the separate self, direct perception of unity, and the sense that "mind" is the ground of reality — not a byproduct of neurons firing.

## The Angle
This post doesn't just say "science proves spirituality." That's lazy and already everywhere. Instead, it follows a specific thread: the DMN research from Judson Brewer, Matthew Johnson, and Robin Carhart-Harris maps *precisely* onto the Hermetic hierarchy of mental planes described in the Corpus Hermeticum. The post walks through three specific correspondences:

1. **The brain as filter, not generator** — DMN suppression correlates with ego dissolution, matching the Hermetic teaching that individual mind is a localized expression of universal Mind.
2. **Meditation as mental transmutation** — Brewer's research on habit loops (trigger → behavior → reward) shows the brain literally rewires when you observe without reacting — which is the Hermetic practice of mental alchemy.
3. **Psychedelics as accidental initiation** — Johns Hopkins research shows psilocybin reliably produces "mystical-type experiences" rated as among the most meaningful in participants' lives. The content of those experiences matches the Corpus Hermeticum's description of Nous (Divine Mind) contact.

They'll see meditation and altered states not as relaxation hacks, but as direct access to the ground of consciousness — and they'll have the neuroscience to back it up when skeptics push back.
