## AI Detection

**Score:** 83% AI probability (class: `ai`)
**Breakdown:** ai: 79% | human: 14% | ai_humanized: 4% | light_edited: 3%

This is a high score. However, after careful reading, the detection is likely driven by **structural patterns** rather than actual AI-generated content. The voice is too specific, too opinionated, and too well-sourced to be raw AI output. The detector is likely flagging:

### Patterns triggering the detector:

**1. Em dash overuse (primary suspect)**
The post uses em dashes heavily — both for parenthetical asides and for the "X — Y" emphasis construction. Count: approximately 22 em dashes across ~1800 words. That's roughly one every 80 words, which is above normal human usage. Examples:

- Line 1: "Your Brain Filters Reality — Hermes Told You That 2,000 Years Ago"
- "there's a network of brain regions — neuroscientists call it the Default Mode Network, or DMN — that lights up"
- "through meditation, through breathwork, through psilocybin, through flow states, through a dozen other methods"
- "that trigger-behavior-reward cycle that runs your life more than you'd like to admit"
- "The brain has been reorganized — transmuted — through the simple, repeatable act"
- "He received knowledge directly, not through reasoning or sensory input, but through a kind of knowing that preceded both."

Some of these are effective. The breathwork/psilocybin/flow states one uses em dashes to build rhythm. But others could be commas or periods. The "reorganized — transmuted" one in particular feels like an AI tell.

**2. "Not X. Y." negative parallelism pattern**
Used 5-6 times:
- "Not suppressing it. Not fighting it."
- "Not metaphorically."
- "It's not 'woo.'"
- "Not empty. Full."
- "This isn't mysticism. It's neuroscience wearing a very old hat."

These are individually fine and fit the voice. But clustering them triggers detectors.

**3. Emphasis significance (mild)**
- "it's the kind of finding that should be on every newspaper front page" — slightly promotional

**4. Repetition of "Not metaphorically"**
Used twice (in the DMN section and the meditation section). Minor but detectable as a pattern.

## Specific Line-Reference Notes

These are **suggestions, not blockers:**

1. **"It's the kind of finding that should be on every newspaper front page"** (The filter you didn't know you had section) — This is the one sentence that reads slightly AI-hyped. The rest of the post earns its claims; this one asserts significance without earning it. Consider cutting or rewriting: e.g., "It's the kind of finding that changes how you think about your own mind."

2. **"The brain has been reorganized — transmuted — through the simple, repeatable act"** (Sitting still as alchemy section) — The "X — Y" construction here is the most AI-flaggable pattern in the post. The word "transmuted" does important conceptual work connecting to the Hermetic frame, so don't lose it. Consider: "The brain has been reorganized — or, in Hermetic terms, transmuted — through..." or just "The brain has been transmuted through..."

3. **"Not metaphorically"** used twice — Once in the DMN section, once in the meditation section. This is clearly intentional voice repetition, but it reads as a formula to detectors. Consider varying: "Literally" or "Actually" or just letting the context make it clear the second time.

4. **Em dashes generally** — Not a specific line, but overall: about 5-6 of the 22 em dashes could be commas or periods without losing the conversational rhythm. This would bring the density closer to normal human usage and likely lower the AI score significantly.
