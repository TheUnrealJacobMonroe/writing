## Issues

### 1. Check engine light metaphor used twice
The metaphor appears in "The Signal You Keep Trying to Silence" and again in "The Soul Audit" ("you don't have to close it all at once... just stop taping over the light"). The second use undercuts the first. Keep it in the introduction to the metaphor; cut or replace the callback.

### 2. "You went on that trip to Portugal" — too specific
This is a fabricated detail pretending to know the reader's life. It works as a rhetorical move, but it's risky — some readers will find it presumptuous. Softening to something less destination-specific ("You went on that trip you'd been planning for months") reduces the chance of breaking immersion.

### 3. The closing section runs long
"The Feeling Is Not the Enemy" and the final two paragraphs do overlapping work. The last three paragraphs all say "the dissatisfaction is intelligence, not a flaw." Could compress to two.

### 4. Minor: "align with"
One instance of "align with your design." This is borderline AI vocabulary but reads naturally in context. Not a blocker.

The post is strong. It's within word count, clean on anti-AI checks, accurate enough on Hermetic content, and reads like a human wrote it. The two uses of the check engine light metaphor are the most actionable fix, but they don't warrant a fail.
